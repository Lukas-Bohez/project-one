## Why Public Domain Matters

Public domain works are creative materials that are no longer protected by copyright law. This means they can be used freely for any purpose - commercial or non-commercial - without permission or payment.

### Key Benefits:
- **Free Access**: No copyright restrictions or licensing fees
- **Educational Value**: Perfect for schools, universities, and self-learners
- **Creative Freedom**: Remix, adapt, and build upon existing works
- **Cultural Preservation**: Keep classic works alive and accessible
- **Innovation**: Foundation for new creative works and technologies

### How Works Enter the Public Domain:
- **US Copyright Law**: Works published before 1929 are generally public domain
- **International Variations**: Copyright terms vary by country
- **Author's Death**: Many countries use "life plus 70 years" rule
- **Government Works**: Often automatically public domain
- **Voluntary Release**: Some creators choose to release works early
